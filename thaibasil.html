🌿Thai Basil

Thai Basil is an aromatic herb with a slightly spicy, licorice-like flavor. It is commonly used in Asian cuisine, especially in stir-fries and soups. It grows well in warm climates and is suitable for indoor pots, balconies, and small home gardens.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs 6–8 hours of sunlight daily but can tolerate partial shade.

💧 Watering

Keep soil moist but not soggy. Water when the top layer of soil feels dry.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 20°C – 32°C. Thrives in warm conditions.

🪴 Soil

Use well-draining soil enriched with compost or organic matter.

✂️ Harvest

Harvest regularly once mature. Trim the top leaves to promote bushier growth.

🍝 Uses

Used in Asian dishes, stir-fries, soups, herbal tea, garnish, and home gardening

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Fill a pot with fertile, well-draining soil.
  2. Sow seeds about 0.5 cm deep and water gently.
  3. Place in a sunny area with at least 6 hours of sunlight.
  4. Seeds usually germinate within 5–10 days.
  5. Thin seedlings or transfer to larger pots when they grow bigger.
  6. Start harvesting once the plant has enough leaves.

❤️ Benefits

  • Rich in antioxidants and essential nutrients
  • Adds strong flavor to dishes naturally
  • Easy to grow in warm climates
  • Pleasant aroma for home gardens
  • Can help repel some insects
  • Suitable for beginners and small farming projects

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Yellow Leaves

Cause: Overwatering or poor drainage.
Solution: Reduce watering and improve drainage.

2. Wilting

Cause: Underwatering or heat stress.
Solution: Water properly and provide shade if needed.

3. Leaf Spots / Fungal Disease

Cause: High humidity and wet leaves.
Solution: Improve airflow and avoid watering leaves.

4. Insects (Aphids / Whiteflies)

Cause: Common pests attacking leaves.
Solution: Use mild soap spray or remove manually.

5. Slow Growth

Cause: Lack of sunlight or nutrients.
Solution: Move to brighter area and add compost.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Pinch off flowers to encourage leaf growth
  • Prune regularly for a fuller plant
  • Avoid overwatering
  • Use compost or fertilizer monthly
  • Place in warm, sunny location
  • Remove damaged or yellow leaves

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Grow indoors or on balconies
  • Use in cooking Asian dishes
  • Make herbal drinks or tea
  • Sell fresh herbs locally
  • Start a small herb garden
  • Share or trade with friends and neighbors

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Ocimum basilicum var. thyrsiflora
  • Family: Mint family (Lamiaceae)
  • Popular in Thai and Southeast Asian cuisine
  • Stronger flavor than Sweet Basil
  • Grows well in Malaysia’s climate
  • Regular harvesting improves plant growth
