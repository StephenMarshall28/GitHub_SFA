🍅 Tomato

Tomato is a popular fruit vegetable widely used in cooking. It grows well in warm climates and can be easily planted in pots, balconies, or home gardens. Tomatoes produce juicy fruits and are suitable for beginners.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily for healthy growth and fruit production.

💧 Watering

Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy. Water regularly and evenly.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 20°C – 30°C. Avoid extreme heat or cold.

🪴 Soil

Use rich, well-draining soil with compost or organic matter.

✂️ Harvest

Harvest when fruits are fully colored (red, yellow, or depending on variety).

🍝 Uses

Used in cooking, salads, sauces, juices, and many dishes

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Fill a pot with fertile, well-draining soil.
  2. Sow seeds about 0.5–1 cm deep.
  3. Water gently and keep soil moist.
  4. Place in a sunny area with at least 6 hours of sunlight.
  5. Seeds usually germinate within 5–10 days.
  6. Transfer seedlings to larger pots as they grow.
  7. Provide support (stakes) as the plant grows taller.

❤️ Benefits

  • Rich in vitamins A, C, and antioxidants
  • Supports healthy skin and immune system
  • Easy to grow at home
  • Produces many fruits over time
  • Suitable for beginners
  • Can be grown in small spaces

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Yellow Leaves

Cause: Overwatering or nutrient deficiency.
Solution: Adjust watering and add fertilizer.

2. Blossom End Rot

Cause: Calcium deficiency or uneven watering.
Solution: Maintain consistent watering and add calcium.

3. Leaf Spots / Fungal Disease

Cause: High humidity and wet leaves.
Solution: Improve airflow and avoid watering leaves.

4. Insects (Aphids / Whiteflies)

Cause: Common pests feeding on plant sap.
Solution: Use mild soap spray or natural pesticide.

5. Slow Growth

Cause: Lack of sunlight or poor soil nutrients.
Solution: Provide full sunlight and enrich soil.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Provide support using stakes or cages
  • Water consistently and evenly
  • Use fertilizer regularly
  • Ensure full sunlight exposure
  • Remove damaged or yellow leaves
  • Prune excess branches for better airflow

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Use fresh in salads and cooking
  • Make sauces, juices, and soups
  • Grow in pots or home gardens
  • Sell fresh tomatoes locally
  • Start a small vegetable garden
  • Use in daily meals

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Solanum lycopersicum
  • Family: Solanaceae
  • Technically a fruit but used as a vegetable
  • Comes in many varieties (cherry, plum, etc.)
  • Thrives in warm climates like Malaysia
  • Needs support as it grows taller
