🌳 Durian

Durian is known as the “King of Fruits” and is famous for its strong smell and rich, creamy taste. It grows on large trees and thrives in tropical climates like Malaysia. Durian requires space, proper care, and time to produce fruits.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs full sunlight for healthy growth and fruit development.

💧 Watering

Water regularly, especially during dry periods. Keep soil moist but not waterlogged.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 24°C – 32°C. Prefers warm and humid conditions.

🪴 Soil

Deep, fertile, well-draining soil rich in organic matter.

✂️ Harvest

Fruits fall naturally from the tree when ripe. Collect promptly.

🍝 Uses

Used for fresh eating, desserts, sweets, and local dishes

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Select a large area with fertile, well-draining soil.
  2. Plant durian seedlings or grafted plants into the ground.
  3. Water thoroughly after planting.
  4. Ensure full sunlight exposure.
  5. Provide enough space for the tree to grow large.
  6. Maintain regular watering and fertilization.
  7. Fruiting may take several years.

❤️ Benefits

  • Rich in energy, vitamins, and minerals
  • Popular and valuable fruit in local markets
  • High economic value for farmers
  • Provides shade and greenery
  • Unique taste loved by many people
  • Suitable for long-term planting projects

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Root Rot

Cause: Poor drainage or overwatering.
Solution: Improve soil drainage and avoid excess water.

2. Leaf Blight

Cause: Fungal infection.
Solution: Remove infected leaves and apply treatment.

3. Fruit Drop

Cause: Stress, lack of nutrients, or water issues.
Solution: Ensure proper watering and fertilization.

4. Pests (Borers / Aphids)

Cause: Insects damaging tree and fruit.
Solution: Use pest control methods and monitor regularly.

5. Slow Growth

Cause: Poor soil or lack of nutrients.
Solution: Improve soil quality and apply fertilizer.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Water regularly, especially during dry seasons
  • Apply fertilizer for better growth
  • Ensure good drainage to prevent root rot
  • Provide enough space for tree expansion
  • Remove dead or diseased branches
  • Monitor for pests and diseases

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Eat fresh durian or use in desserts
  • Sell durians in local markets
  • Start a small fruit farm
  • Use for food products like cakes and ice cream
  • Grow as long-term investment crop
  • Share harvest with family or community

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Durio zibethinus
  • Family: Malvaceae
  • Known as the “King of Fruits”
  • Strong smell but rich, creamy taste
  • Requires several years to bear fruit
  • Commonly grown in Malaysia and Southeast Asia
