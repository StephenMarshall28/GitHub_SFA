🌿Sweet Basil

Sweet Basil is a popular aromatic herb known for its fresh green leaves and pleasant fragrance. It is widely used in cooking, especially in pasta, salads, soups, and sauces. Basil grows quickly in warm climates and is suitable for home gardens, pots, balconies, or small farming projects.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily for healthy growth and strong flavor.

💧 Watering

Water regularly to keep soil slightly moist. Avoid overwatering or leaving roots in soggy soil.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 20°C – 30°C. Protect from cold weather below 10°C.

🪴 Soil

Use well-draining fertile soil rich in compost or organic matter. Soil should stay moist but not waterlogged.

✂️ Harvest

Can be harvested 4–6 weeks after planting. Pick leaves regularly and trim the top stems to encourage bushier growth.

🍝 Uses

Cooking herb for pasta, pizza, pesto, soups, salads, herbal tea, natural fragrance plant, decorative herb in gardens, companion plant for vegetables

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Fill a pot or garden bed with fertile well-draining soil.
  2. Sow basil seeds about 0.5 cm deep. Water gently after planting.
  3. Place in sunny area with 6–8 hours sunlight.
  4. Seeds usually germinate in 5–10 days.
  5. Thin seedlings or transfer to larger pots when bigger.
  6. Start harvesting once plant has enough leaves.

❤️ Benefits

  • Rich in vitamins A, K, and antioxidants
  • Adds fresh flavor to food naturally
  • Pleasant aroma around home garden
  • Easy for beginners to grow
  • Can help attract pollinators like bees
  • Suitable for small home farming projects

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Grow in pots at home
  • Sell fresh leaves locally
  • Make pesto sauce or herbal drinks
  • Use in cooking daily meals
  • Use in community garden projects
  • Trade herbs with neighbors or local markets

🌿 Care Tips

  • Pinch off flower buds to keep leaf production high
  • Prune regularly for bushier growth
  • Do not overwater
  • Use compost monthly for better growth
  • Keep in sunny and warm place
  • Remove yellow or damaged leaves

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Yellow Leaves

Cause: Overwatering or poor drainage.
Solution: Reduce watering and improve soil drainage.

2. Wilting

Cause: Underwatering or root damage.
Solution: Water properly and check roots.

3. Black Spots / Fungal Disease

Cause: Too much moisture on leaves.
Solution: Improve airflow and avoid watering leaves late evening.

4. Insects (Aphids / Whiteflies)

Cause: Common garden pests.
Solution: Spray mild soap water or remove by hand.

5. Slow Growth

Cause: Lack of sunlight or poor soil nutrients.
Solution: Move to sunnier area and add compost.

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Ocimum basilicum
  • Family: Mint family (Lamiaceae)
  • Fast-growing herb suitable for Malaysia climate
  • Companion plant near tomatoes and peppers
  • Regular harvesting helps plant grow more leaves
  • Strong aroma may help reduce some insects nearby
