🍈 Cempedak

Cempedak is a tropical fruit similar to jackfruit, known for its sweet taste and strong aroma. It grows on large trees and is commonly found in Southeast Asia. The fruit is popular for fresh eating and fried snacks.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs full sunlight for healthy growth and fruit production.

💧 Watering

Water regularly to keep soil moist, especially during dry periods.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 24°C – 32°C. Thrives in warm and humid climates.

🪴 Soil

Use fertile, well-draining soil rich in organic matter.

✂️ Harvest

Harvest when fruit becomes soft and produces a strong, sweet aroma.

🍝 Uses

Used for fresh eating, fried snacks, desserts, and local dishes

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Select a planting area with rich, well-draining soil.
  2. Plant cempedak seedlings into the ground.
  3. Water thoroughly after planting.
  4. Ensure full sunlight exposure.
  5. Provide enough space for tree growth.
  6. Maintain regular watering and fertilization.
  7. Fruiting may take several years.

❤️ Benefits

  • Rich in carbohydrates and energy
  • Popular and valuable local fruit
  • Can be used in various dishes
  • Suitable for small farming
  • Provides shade in gardens
  • Unique taste and aroma

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Root Rot

Cause: Poor drainage or overwatering.
Solution: Improve soil drainage and reduce watering.

2. Leaf Yellowing

Cause: Nutrient deficiency.
Solution: Apply fertilizer regularly.

3. Fruit Rot

Cause: Fungal infection.
Solution: Remove affected fruits and improve hygiene.

4. Pests (Fruit Flies / Borers)

Cause: Insects attacking fruit.
Solution: Use protective covering and pest control.

5. Slow Growth

Cause: Poor soil or lack of nutrients.
Solution: Improve soil and fertilize regularly.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Water regularly, especially in dry weather
  • Use fertilizer to support growth
  • Ensure good drainage
  • Provide enough space for tree expansion
  • Remove damaged leaves and fruits
  • Monitor for pests and diseases

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Eat fresh fruit or make fried snacks
  • Use in desserts and local dishes
  • Grow in home gardens or farms
  • Sell fruits in local markets
  • Start a small fruit business
  • Share harvest with family and friends

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Artocarpus integer
  • Family: Moraceae
  • Related to jackfruit
  • Strong aroma and sweet taste
  • Common in Malaysia and Southeast Asia
  • Requires several years to produce fruit
