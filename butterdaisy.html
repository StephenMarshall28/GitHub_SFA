🌻 Butter Daisy

Butter Daisy is a cheerful flowering plant known for its soft yellow daisy-like blooms. It is commonly grown as an ornamental plant in gardens, pots, and landscapes. Butter Daisy is easy to grow and adds a bright, warm look to outdoor spaces.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs full sunlight (6–8 hours daily) for strong growth and continuous flowering.

💧 Watering

Water moderately and allow soil to dry slightly between watering sessions.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 18°C – 30°C in warm and sunny environments.

🪴 Soil

Use well-draining fertile soil with organic compost for best flowering results.

✂️ Blooming

Produces flowers throughout warm seasons with proper care and sunlight.

🌸 Uses

Used for garden decoration, landscaping, flower beds, and potted displays.

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Prepare loose and well-draining soil in pots or garden beds.
  2. Plant Butter Daisy seeds or seedlings lightly into the soil.
  3. Water gently after planting.
  4. Place in a sunny location with good airflow.
  5. Seeds usually germinate within 7–12 days.
  6. Transplant seedlings when they are strong enough.
  7. Remove faded flowers to encourage continuous blooming.

❤️ Benefits

  • Adds bright yellow color to gardens
  • Easy to grow for beginners
  • Attracts butterflies and bees
  • Suitable for pots and garden beds
  • Improves outdoor landscape appearance
  • Long flowering period in warm seasons

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Root Rot

Cause: Overwatering or poor drainage.
Solution: Reduce watering and improve soil drainage.

2. Powdery Mildew

Cause: High humidity and poor airflow.
Solution: Improve ventilation and avoid wetting leaves.

3. Aphids / Insects

Cause: Common garden pests.
Solution: Use natural pest control or remove manually.

4. Weak Flowering

Cause: Lack of sunlight or nutrients.
Solution: Provide full sunlight and fertilize regularly.

5. Yellow Leaves

Cause: Overwatering or poor soil nutrients.
Solution: Adjust watering and improve soil quality.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Place in full sunlight for best flowering
  • Do not overwater the plant
  • Remove dead flowers regularly
  • Use compost for healthier growth
  • Ensure good airflow around plant
  • Protect from heavy rainfall damage

🌻 What You Can Do

  • Grow in home gardens or balconies
  • Use for landscaping decoration
  • Decorate flower pots and patios
  • Attract pollinators like bees
  • Use in school gardening projects
  • Sell as ornamental flowering plants

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Melampodium divaricatum
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Known for bright yellow daisy-like flowers
  • Native to tropical regions of the Americas
  • Low-maintenance ornamental plant
  • Blooms continuously in warm climates
