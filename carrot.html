🥕 Carrot

Carrot is a nutritious root vegetable known for its bright orange color and crunchy texture. It is commonly used in salads, soups, fried rice, juices, and healthy meals. Carrots are easy to grow in loose soil and are suitable for home gardens, containers, and small farming projects.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily for healthy root development.

💧 Watering

Water regularly to keep soil moist but not overly wet. Avoid waterlogged soil.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 16°C – 24°C. Cooler temperatures help improve root quality.

🪴 Soil

Use loose, sandy, well-draining soil free from rocks to allow straight root growth.

✂️ Harvest

Can be harvested 2–3 months after planting depending on carrot variety and size.

🥗 Uses

Used in salads, soups, juices, fried rice, cakes, healthy snacks, and cooking dishes.

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Prepare loose and fertile soil with good drainage.
  2. Sow carrot seeds directly into the soil about 1 cm deep.
  3. Water gently after planting to keep soil moist.
  4. Place in sunny area with enough sunlight daily.
  5. Thin seedlings when they grow bigger to prevent overcrowding.
  6. Harvest carrots once roots reach suitable size.

❤️ Benefits

  • Rich in Vitamin A and antioxidants
  • Supports healthy eyesight
  • Good for healthy diet and nutrition
  • Easy vegetable for beginners to grow
  • Can be grown in pots or garden beds
  • Suitable for small farming projects

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Root Rot

Cause: Overwatering or poor drainage.
Solution: Improve drainage and avoid excessive watering.

2. Forked or Twisted Roots

Cause: Rocky or compact soil.
Solution: Use loose sandy soil without stones.

3. Yellow Leaves

Cause: Nutrient deficiency or poor watering.
Solution: Add compost and water properly.

4. Insects (Aphids / Carrot Flies)

Cause: Common garden pests.
Solution: Remove damaged leaves and use natural pest control.

5. Slow Growth

Cause: Lack of sunlight or poor soil nutrients.
Solution: Move to sunnier location and improve soil fertility.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Keep soil loose and weed-free
  • Water consistently but avoid overwatering
  • Thin seedlings for better root growth
  • Add compost for healthier carrots
  • Provide enough sunlight daily
  • Harvest before roots become too old and hard

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Grow carrots in pots or gardens
  • Sell fresh carrots locally
  • Make carrot juice or healthy meals
  • Use in cooking daily dishes
  • Start small vegetable farming projects
  • Share harvest with family or community

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Daucus carota
  • Family: Apiaceae family
  • Root vegetable commonly grown worldwide
  • Available in orange, purple, yellow, and red varieties
  • Fresh carrots can be stored for several weeks
  • Popular healthy snack for all ages
