🌿 Mint

Mint is a fast-growing herb known for its refreshing aroma and cooling taste. It spreads quickly and is very easy to grow, making it perfect for indoor pots, balconies, and small home gardens. Mint is commonly used in drinks, desserts, and cooking.

☀️ Sunlight

Prefers 4–6 hours of sunlight. Can grow well in partial shade.

💧 Watering

Keep soil consistently moist. Do not let the soil dry out completely.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 18°C – 30°C. Tolerates slightly cooler conditions.

🪴 Soil

Use moist, well-draining soil rich in organic matter.

✂️ Harvest

Harvest regularly by cutting stems to encourage fuller and bushier growth.

🍝 Uses

Used in drinks, desserts, cooking, herbal tea, and natural remedies

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Prepare a pot with moist, well-draining soil.
  2. Plant mint cuttings or seeds about 0.5 cm deep.
  3. Water gently and keep soil consistently moist.
  4. Place in an area with partial sunlight.
  5. Mint grows quickly and spreads within a few weeks.
  6. Start harvesting once the plant is well established.

❤️ Benefits

  • Helps refresh breath naturally
  • Rich in antioxidants
  • Easy to grow and maintain
  • Provides cooling effect in drinks and food
  • Can help repel some insects
  • Suitable for beginners

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Yellow Leaves

Cause: Overwatering or poor drainage.
Solution: Improve drainage and reduce watering.

2. Wilting

Cause: Dry soil or lack of water.
Solution: Water regularly to keep soil moist.

3. Fungal Disease

Cause: High humidity and poor airflow.
Solution: Improve air circulation and avoid overcrowding.

4. Insects (Aphids / Spider Mites)

Cause: Common pests feeding on leaves.
Solution: Use mild soap spray or rinse with water.

5. Overgrowth

Cause: Mint spreads rapidly.
Solution: Trim regularly and grow in controlled containers.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Trim regularly to control growth
  • Keep soil moist at all times
  • Avoid planting with other plants (can spread aggressively)
  • Place in partial sunlight
  • Remove old or damaged leaves
  • Use containers to prevent spreading

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Make refreshing drinks like mint tea or lemonade
  • Use in desserts and cooking
  • Grow indoors in small pots
  • Sell fresh mint leaves
  • Create herbal remedies
  • Start a small herb garden

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Mentha
  • Family: Mint family (Lamiaceae)
  • Very fast-growing and spreading plant
  • Best grown in containers to control growth
  • Strong refreshing aroma
  • Suitable for Malaysia’s warm climate
