🌼 Frangipani

Frangipani is a beautiful tropical flowering plant known for its fragrant, soft-petaled flowers. It is commonly found in warm climates and is often planted in gardens, temples, and landscapes. Frangipani flowers are highly valued for their scent and decorative appearance.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs full sunlight (6–8 hours daily) for healthy growth and flowering.

💧 Watering

Water moderately. Allow soil to dry slightly between watering.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 22°C – 32°C. Prefers warm tropical climates.

🪴 Soil

Use well-draining sandy or loamy soil with good organic content.

✂️ Blooming

Flowers bloom mainly during warm seasons with proper sunlight.

🌸 Uses

Used for decoration, landscaping, religious sites, and fragrance production.

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Prepare well-draining sandy soil in a sunny location.
  2. Plant frangipani cuttings or seedlings into the soil.
  3. Water lightly after planting.
  4. Place in a full sunlight area for best growth.
  5. Allow soil to dry between watering sessions.
  6. Support young plants if needed until established.
  7. Prune branches to encourage more flowering.

❤️ Benefits

  • Produces highly fragrant flowers
  • Beautiful ornamental plant for gardens
  • Suitable for tropical landscaping
  • Low maintenance once established
  • Symbolic flower in many cultures
  • Attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Root Rot

Cause: Overwatering or poor drainage.
Solution: Reduce watering and improve soil drainage.

2. Fungal Infection

Cause: High humidity and poor airflow.
Solution: Improve ventilation and remove affected parts.

3. Aphids / Mealybugs

Cause: Common sap-sucking insects.
Solution: Use natural pest control or insecticidal soap.

4. Leaf Drop

Cause: Water stress or seasonal changes.
Solution: Maintain proper watering and care routine.

5. Poor Flowering

Cause: Lack of sunlight or nutrients.
Solution: Provide full sunlight and fertilize properly.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Place in full sunlight for best flowering
  • Do not overwater the plant
  • Prune regularly to shape the plant
  • Use fertilizer during growing season
  • Allow soil to dry before watering again
  • Protect from heavy waterlogging

🌸 What You Can Do

  • Grow as decorative garden tree
  • Use for landscaping and parks
  • Place near homes for fragrance
  • Use in cultural or religious decoration
  • Take cut flowers for decoration
  • Sell ornamental plants locally

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Plumeria
  • Family: Apocynaceae
  • Known for strong sweet fragrance
  • Common in tropical and subtropical regions
  • Often used in leis and cultural garlands
  • Can grow as shrub or small tree
