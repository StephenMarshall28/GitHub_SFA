🌵 Aloe Vera

Aloe Vera is a succulent plant known for its thick, fleshy leaves filled with soothing gel. It is widely used for skin care and medicinal purposes. Aloe Vera is low-maintenance and perfect for indoor pots and small spaces.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs bright indirect sunlight. Can tolerate some direct sunlight but avoid intense heat.

💧 Watering

Water sparingly. Allow soil to dry completely between watering.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 18°C – 30°C. Avoid cold temperatures below 10°C.

🪴 Soil

Use sandy, well-draining soil such as cactus or succulent mix.

✂️ Harvest

Harvest by cutting mature outer leaves when needed.

🍝 Uses

Used for skin care, burns, wounds, and natural remedies

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Prepare a pot with sandy, well-draining soil.
  2. Plant Aloe Vera offsets (small baby plants) into the soil.
  3. Water lightly after planting.
  4. Place in a bright area with indirect sunlight.
  5. Avoid overwatering during early growth.
  6. Allow plant to establish before harvesting leaves.

❤️ Benefits

  • Soothes burns and skin irritation
  • Natural moisturizer for skin
  • Easy to grow and maintain
  • Requires minimal watering
  • Useful for home remedies
  • Ideal for indoor environments

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Root Rot

Cause: Overwatering or poor drainage.
Solution: Reduce watering and use well-draining soil.

2. Brown or Soft Leaves

Cause: Too much water or rot.
Solution: Allow soil to dry and trim affected parts.

3. Dry or Thin Leaves

Cause: Underwatering.
Solution: Water slightly more frequently.

4. Sunburn

Cause: Excessive direct sunlight.
Solution: Move to indirect light.

5. Slow Growth

Cause: Lack of sunlight or nutrients.
Solution: Provide better light and occasional feeding.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Do not overwater
  • Ensure good drainage
  • Place in bright, indirect sunlight
  • Use cactus or succulent soil
  • Remove damaged leaves
  • Repot when plant becomes crowded

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Use gel for skin care and burns
  • Grow indoors as decorative plant
  • Make natural home remedies
  • Share or propagate plants easily
  • Use as low-maintenance houseplant
  • Start a small indoor garden

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Aloe vera
  • Family: Asphodelaceae
  • Succulent plant that stores water in leaves
  • Very drought-tolerant
  • Commonly used in cosmetic and health products
  • Produces small offsets for easy propagation
