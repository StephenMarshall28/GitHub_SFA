🟣 Mangosteen

Mangosteen is a tropical fruit tree famous for its sweet white flesh and thick purple rind. Often called the “Queen of Fruits,” mangosteen is popular in Southeast Asia and grows best in warm, humid climates. The fruit is enjoyed fresh and is suitable for orchards, home gardens, and tropical farming projects.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs partial to full sunlight daily for healthy growth and fruit production.

💧 Watering

Requires regular watering to keep soil moist, especially during dry weather.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 25°C – 35°C in warm and humid tropical climates.

🪴 Soil

Use fertile, deep, well-draining soil rich in organic matter.

✂️ Harvest

Trees usually produce fruits after several years. Harvest when fruits turn dark purple.

🍹 Uses

Consumed fresh, used in desserts, juices, smoothies, and tropical fruit dishes.

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Prepare fertile soil with proper drainage and organic compost.
  2. Plant mangosteen seeds or young seedlings carefully into the ground.
  3. Water regularly to maintain soil moisture.
  4. Place in warm area with partial sunlight during early growth.
  5. Remove weeds and provide fertilizer regularly.
  6. Harvest fruits once they become fully dark purple and ripe.

❤️ Benefits

  • Rich in antioxidants and vitamins
  • Popular tropical fruit with sweet flavor
  • Can provide shade in gardens and orchards
  • Suitable for tropical farming projects
  • Fresh fruits are highly valued in markets
  • Can be used in healthy desserts and drinks

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Yellow Leaves

Cause: Nutrient deficiency or poor soil condition.
Solution: Add compost and balanced fertilizer regularly.

2. Root Rot

Cause: Excessive watering and poor drainage.
Solution: Improve soil drainage and reduce overwatering.

3. Slow Growth

Cause: Lack of nutrients or unsuitable climate.
Solution: Provide proper fertilizer and warm environment.

4. Insects (Ants / Fruit Borers)

Cause: Common tropical fruit pests.
Solution: Keep orchard clean and monitor fruits regularly.

5. Fruit Cracking

Cause: Irregular watering or sudden heavy rain.
Solution: Maintain consistent watering schedule.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Water consistently during dry seasons
  • Use organic compost for healthier growth
  • Protect young plants from strong sunlight
  • Keep planting area free from weeds
  • Prune damaged branches when necessary
  • Monitor fruits regularly for pests

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Grow mangosteen trees in orchards
  • Sell fresh mangosteen fruits locally
  • Make tropical fruit drinks and desserts
  • Use as decorative tropical garden tree
  • Start small fruit farming projects
  • Share fruits with family and community

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Garcinia mangostana
  • Family: Clusiaceae family
  • Known as the “Queen of Fruits”
  • Native to Southeast Asia tropical regions
  • Trees can grow tall and live for many years
  • Fruit contains soft white segments inside thick purple skin
