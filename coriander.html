🌿 Coriander (Cilantro)

Coriander, also known as cilantro, is a popular herb used for its fresh leaves and seeds. It has a light, citrusy flavor and is commonly used in many cuisines. It grows quickly and is suitable for indoor pots, balconies, and small home gardens.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs 4–6 hours of sunlight daily. Prefers partial sunlight.

💧 Watering

Keep soil lightly moist. Avoid overwatering or letting the soil dry out completely.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 17°C – 27°C. Prefers cooler conditions compared to other herbs.

🪴 Soil

Use loose, well-draining soil rich in organic matter.

✂️ Harvest

Harvest leaves early before the plant flowers for the best flavor.

🍝 Uses

Used in cooking, garnishing, salads, soups, and as a spice (seeds)

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Fill a pot with loose, well-draining soil.
  2. Sow coriander seeds about 0.5–1 cm deep.
  3. Water gently and keep soil moist.
  4. Place in an area with partial sunlight.
  5. Seeds usually germinate within 7–14 days.
  6. Start harvesting leaves before flowering begins.

❤️ Benefits

  • Rich in vitamins A, C, and K
  • Adds fresh flavor to food naturally
  • Supports digestion
  • Easy and fast to grow
  • Suitable for small home gardens
  • Can be used both as herb and spice

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Yellow Leaves

Cause: Overwatering or poor drainage.
Solution: Reduce watering and improve soil drainage.

2. Bolting (Early Flowering)

Cause: High temperature or stress.
Solution: Keep plant in cooler conditions and harvest early.

3. Wilting

Cause: Underwatering.
Solution: Water regularly to keep soil moist.

4. Insects (Aphids)

Cause: Pest infestation on leaves.
Solution: Use mild soap spray or rinse with water.

5. Slow Growth

Cause: Poor soil or lack of sunlight.
Solution: Improve soil nutrients and provide adequate light.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Harvest regularly to prevent early flowering
  • Keep soil consistently moist
  • Avoid excessive heat exposure
  • Use nutrient-rich soil
  • Provide partial sunlight
  • Remove damaged leaves

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Use fresh leaves in cooking and garnishing
  • Grow indoors in pots
  • Harvest seeds for spice use
  • Sell fresh herbs locally
  • Start a small herb garden
  • Use in homemade recipes

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Coriandrum sativum
  • Family: Apiaceae
  • Both leaves (cilantro) and seeds (coriander) are used
  • Prefers slightly cooler environments
  • Fast-growing herb with short lifecycle
  • Popular in many global cuisines
