🌶️ Bird’s Eye Chili

Bird’s Eye Chili, also known as chili padi, is a small but very spicy chili commonly used in Southeast Asian cooking. It grows well in warm climates and is suitable for pots, balconies, and home gardens.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily for strong growth and fruit production.

💧 Watering

Water regularly to keep soil moist but not soggy. Avoid overwatering.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 25°C – 35°C. Thrives in hot and humid conditions.

🪴 Soil

Use fertile, well-draining soil rich in organic matter.

✂️ Harvest

Harvest when chilies turn green, red, or desired color. Regular picking encourages more growth.

🍝 Uses

Used in spicy dishes, sambal, sauces, cooking, and food seasoning

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Fill a pot with fertile, well-draining soil.
  2. Sow seeds about 0.5–1 cm deep.
  3. Water gently and keep soil moist.
  4. Place in a sunny area with at least 6 hours of sunlight.
  5. Seeds usually germinate within 7–14 days.
  6. Transfer to larger pots when seedlings grow bigger.
  7. Start harvesting once fruits appear and mature.

❤️ Benefits

  • Rich in vitamin C and antioxidants
  • Enhances flavor with natural spiciness
  • Easy to grow in warm climates
  • Can boost appetite
  • Suitable for small home farming
  • Produces many fruits over time

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Yellow Leaves

Cause: Overwatering or nutrient deficiency.
Solution: Adjust watering and add fertilizer.

2. Flower Drop

Cause: High temperature or inconsistent watering.
Solution: Maintain stable watering and reduce heat stress.

3. Leaf Curl

Cause: Pests or viral infection.
Solution: Remove affected leaves and control pests.

4. Insects (Aphids / Whiteflies)

Cause: Common pests feeding on plant sap.
Solution: Use mild soap spray or natural pesticide.

5. Slow Growth

Cause: Lack of sunlight or poor soil nutrients.
Solution: Provide full sunlight and add compost.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Ensure full sunlight exposure
  • Water consistently but avoid overwatering
  • Use fertilizer regularly for better fruiting
  • Support plant if it grows tall
  • Remove damaged leaves and pests
  • Harvest frequently to encourage new fruits

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Use in cooking spicy dishes and sambal
  • Grow in pots or small garden spaces
  • Sell fresh chilies locally
  • Make chili-based sauces
  • Start a small chili farm
  • Use in home cooking daily

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Capsicum frutescens
  • Family: Solanaceae
  • Commonly known as chili padi in Malaysia
  • Very spicy compared to larger chili varieties
  • Thrives in tropical climates
  • Produces many small fruits per plant
