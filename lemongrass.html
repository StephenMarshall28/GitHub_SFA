🌾 Lemongrass

Lemongrass is a tropical herb known for its fresh citrus aroma and flavor. It is widely used in Asian cooking, especially in soups and curries. Lemongrass grows well in warm climates and can be easily planted in pots or home gardens.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs full sunlight (6–8 hours daily) for strong and healthy growth.

💧 Watering

Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. Water regularly.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 25°C – 35°C. Thrives in hot and humid conditions.

🪴 Soil

Use well-draining, fertile soil rich in organic matter.

✂️ Harvest

Harvest stalks when they are thick and mature, usually after a few months of growth.

🍝 Uses

Used in cooking (soups, curries), herbal tea, and traditional remedies

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Fill a pot with fertile, well-draining soil.
  2. Plant lemongrass stalks with roots into the soil.
  3. Water thoroughly and keep soil moist.
  4. Place in a sunny area with full sunlight.
  5. New shoots will start growing within 1–2 weeks.
  6. Allow the plant to grow thicker before harvesting.

❤️ Benefits

  • Rich in antioxidants
  • Adds fresh citrus flavor to dishes
  • Easy to grow in tropical climates
  • Can be used for herbal tea
  • Natural insect-repelling properties
  • Suitable for home gardening

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Yellow Leaves

Cause: Lack of nutrients or poor soil.
Solution: Add compost or fertilizer.

2. Wilting

Cause: Underwatering or heat stress.
Solution: Water regularly and maintain moisture.

3. Root Rot

Cause: Overwatering or poor drainage.
Solution: Improve soil drainage and reduce watering.

4. Insects

Cause: Occasional pest infestation.
Solution: Use natural pest control methods.

5. Slow Growth

Cause: Lack of sunlight or nutrients.
Solution: Provide full sunlight and enrich soil.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Ensure full sunlight exposure
  • Keep soil consistently moist
  • Trim old or dry leaves regularly
  • Use compost for better growth
  • Plant in spacious pots for root expansion
  • Divide clumps when plant becomes too dense

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Use in cooking soups and curries
  • Make herbal tea
  • Grow in pots or home gardens
  • Sell fresh stalks locally
  • Use as natural insect repellent
  • Start a small herb garden

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Cymbopogon citratus
  • Family: Poaceae (grass family)
  • Common in Southeast Asian cuisine
  • Strong lemon-like aroma
  • Thrives in Malaysia’s tropical climate
  • Can be grown from store-bought stalks
