🥒 Cucumber

Cucumber is a fast-growing vine plant that produces refreshing and crunchy fruits. It is commonly grown in home gardens and farms and can be planted in pots with support. Cucumbers thrive well in warm climates and are easy for beginners.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs full sunlight (6–8 hours daily) for healthy growth and fruit production.

💧 Watering

Keep soil consistently moist. Water regularly, especially during hot weather.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 22°C – 32°C. Prefers warm conditions.

🪴 Soil

Use fertile, well-draining soil rich in organic matter.

✂️ Harvest

Harvest when fruits are medium-sized, firm, and green.

🍝 Uses

Used in salads, drinks, pickles, and fresh consumption

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Fill a pot or garden bed with fertile, well-draining soil.
  2. Sow seeds about 1–2 cm deep.
  3. Water gently and keep soil moist.
  4. Place in a sunny area with full sunlight.
  5. Seeds usually germinate within 5–10 days.
  6. Provide support (trellis) for vines to climb.
  7. Start harvesting once fruits develop.

❤️ Benefits

  • High water content, good for hydration
  • Low in calories and healthy
  • Easy and fast to grow
  • Suitable for small gardens and pots
  • Produces multiple fruits
  • Refreshing and nutritious

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Powdery Mildew

Cause: Fungal disease in humid conditions.
Solution: Improve airflow and avoid wet leaves.

2. Yellow Leaves

Cause: Overwatering or nutrient deficiency.
Solution: Adjust watering and add fertilizer.

3. Wilting

Cause: Underwatering or root stress.
Solution: Water regularly and maintain soil moisture.

4. Pests (Aphids / Beetles)

Cause: Insects feeding on leaves and fruits.
Solution: Use natural pest control methods.

5. Bitter Fruits

Cause: Stress from heat or irregular watering.
Solution: Maintain consistent watering and care.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Provide support for climbing vines
  • Water regularly and consistently
  • Ensure full sunlight exposure
  • Use fertilizer for better yield
  • Harvest frequently to encourage more growth
  • Remove damaged leaves

🍃 What You Can Do

  • Eat fresh in salads
  • Make refreshing drinks
  • Use for pickles
  • Grow in pots or home gardens
  • Sell cucumbers locally
  • Start a small vegetable garden

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Cucumis sativus
  • Family: Cucurbitaceae
  • Fast-growing vine plant
  • Needs support like trellis or netting
  • Thrives in warm climates like Malaysia
  • Harvest regularly for continuous production
