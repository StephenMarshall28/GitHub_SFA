🌻 Sunflower

Sunflower is a tall, bright flowering plant known for its large yellow blooms that follow the sun. It is widely grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and fields. Sunflowers are popular for decoration, photography, and attracting pollinators like bees.

☀️ Sunlight

Needs full sunlight (6–8 hours daily) for strong growth and large blooms.

💧 Watering

Water regularly, especially during early growth. Keep soil moist but not soggy.

🌡️ Temperature

Best growth at 20°C – 30°C. Prefers warm and sunny climates.

🪴 Soil

Use well-draining, fertile soil with organic matter.

✂️ Blooming

Flowers bloom within 70–100 days after planting depending on variety.

🌸 Uses

Used for decoration, landscaping, photography, and attracting pollinators

🌱 How To Plant

  1. Prepare a sunny garden area with well-draining soil.
  2. Plant sunflower seeds about 2–3 cm deep.
  3. Water gently after planting.
  4. Place in a location with full sunlight.
  5. Seeds germinate within 7–10 days.
  6. Thin seedlings to allow proper spacing.
  7. Support tall plants if needed.

❤️ Benefits

  • Brightens garden and outdoor spaces
  • Attracts bees and butterflies
  • Easy to grow for beginners
  • Fast-growing flowering plant
  • Improves landscape aesthetics
  • Good educational plant for students

⚠️ Common Diseases & Problems

1. Fungal Leaf Spots

Cause: High humidity and poor airflow.
Solution: Improve ventilation and remove affected leaves.

2. Stem Rot

Cause: Overwatering or poor drainage.
Solution: Reduce watering and improve soil drainage.

3. Pests (Aphids / Caterpillars)

Cause: Insect infestation.
Solution: Use natural pest control or remove manually.

4. Weak Stems

Cause: Lack of sunlight or nutrients.
Solution: Provide full sunlight and fertilizer.

5. Poor Flowering

Cause: Insufficient light or nutrients.
Solution: Improve sunlight exposure and soil quality.

🌿 Care Tips

  • Ensure full sunlight exposure daily
  • Water regularly during early growth
  • Use fertilizer for stronger blooms
  • Provide support for tall plants
  • Remove dead flowers to encourage new growth
  • Keep soil well-drained

🌸 What You Can Do

  • Grow for garden decoration
  • Use for photography and aesthetics
  • Attract bees and butterflies
  • Decorate home or school gardens
  • Use for educational plant projects
  • Sell flowers for decoration

ℹ️ Extra Info

  • Scientific Name: Helianthus annuus
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Known for turning toward sunlight (heliotropism)
  • Popular ornamental flower worldwide
  • Fast-growing annual plant
  • Symbol of happiness and positivity
